About

I completed doctoral research in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Anupam Datta and Matt Fredrikson in the Accountable Systems Lab.

My research centers on the question of what it means for a machine learning explanation to be useful: whether it is stable, faithful, robust to perturbation, and capable of revealing the mechanism behind a model decision.

Earlier work includes visual attribution methods, robust neural network training, explanation methods for deep reinforcement learning, and influence-path abstractions for BERT models.
